EDEN

ABOUT

Jonathon Ng (born 23 December 1995), known professionally as Eden (stylised as EDEN), is an Irish singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, record producer and occasional model. He formerly operated as The Spab Project and later The Eden Project, an alias that was discontinued in 2015. Ng's work as The Eden Project typically featured more conventional styles of electronic dance music such as dubstep and drum and bass, while Eden saw him venturing into a more indie pop style. Ng released six EPs and over 70 songs and remixes as The Eden Project. His work as The Eden Project typically featured more conventional styles of electronic dance music such as dubstep and drum and bass, while EDEN saw him venturing into a more indie pop style. As Eden, he launched his own record label, MCMXCV, on which his debut EP End Credits was released. His second EP, I Think You Think Too Much of Me, was released in August 2016, debuting at No. 43 on the Irish Albums Chart as his first charting music.[6] Eden's debut studio album Vertigo was released on 19 January 2018 and was supported by a world tour. In February 2020 Ng released his sophomore studio album called “no future” which is also followed up by another world tour. “No Future” made it to the top 150 of the all-genres album charts and to the top 10 of the alternative album charts upon one day after release.

As EDEN, Ng started his own label, MCMXCV, which he has released both of his EPs through. His debut EP, End Credits, was released for free through both Seeking Blue Records and MCMXCV on 8 August 2015. End Credits has amassed over 8.6M plays on Soundcloud, as well as over 14M plays on Spotify. On 23 November 2015, Ng released his cover of Billie Jean, which has amassed 1.9M plays on Soundcloud, 3.2M plays on Spotify, and 2.2M plays on MrSuicideSheep's upload on YouTube. On 23 December 2015, Ng released the first video in a new video series titled '365X : 20', where he goes over what happened in the previous year. On 2 February 2016, Ng announced his debut tour The End Credits Tour. With seven dates across six cities and four countries, the tour had dates selling out within the week, with another date being added to NYC due to demand. On 11 March 2016, Ng announced that the whole tour was sold out and on 22 March 2016, he embarked on the tour. During the tour, the lead single off of his next EP, 'sex', an extended version of his hit off The Eden Project, 'XO', as well as a cover of Outkast's 'Hey Ya' you could only download for a period of time by signing up for his mailing list were played. The tour ended 8 April 2016 in San Francisco. On 24 May 2016, Ng uploaded a video titled 'all i want', which was featured as the prologue to his announcement of his sophomore EP, 'i think you think too much of me'. You can later hear the lyrics from this video in the background of 'rock + roll' at the very end. On 2 June 2016, Ng formally announced the sophomore EP, 'i think you think too much of me', here. The EP officially went for pre-order 10 June 2016, with clear and red vinyl being sold alongside cassettes. The first 500 of each sold were signed. On June 9th, 2016, the lead single 'sex' was released along with the official video. Later that week, on the 13 June 2016, he announced his sophomore tour, called 'The Futurebound Tour.' It was later announced that Elohim would be the support in NA and Majik would be the support in EU. The second single off the EP, 'drugs', was released on 14 July 2016. The music video was later released a day before the EP officially dropped. On 18 August 2016, 'i think you think too much of me' officially dropped.

The EP instantly shot up to the #1 Alternative albums spot on the US iTunes charts, as well as debuting in the top 50 of the Irish Albums Chart. On 7 September 2016, Ng released 'drugs, the virtual reality experience' and amassed 1.5M plays on Facebook in just over a week. In the middle of Ng's 'Futurebound' tour, he released the music video for 'rock + roll', shot by Tim Kellner. The tour was completed on 26 Nov. 2016. Ng tweeted out his thank yous the following Monday. On September 27, 2017, Ng's lead single 'start//end' was released on Youtube alongside the official video. A month later on November 7th, 2017, a scavenger hunt took place in 13 cities across the world. At each location, posters contained a secret Spotify link to the single "gold" two days prior to its release. On November 10th, 2017, Ng released the second single 'gold' on Youtube alongside the official video. On December 8th, 2017, Ng released the third single 'crash' to Youtube alongside with an official video. On January 18th, 2018, Ng released the last single, 'float', alongside the official video for his debut album 'Vertigo'. 'Vertigo' was released the next day on January 19th, 2018. On June 29, 2018, Ng releases his single 'about time' with the tracks 'about time' and 'stutter',a month later and he releases another single 'All You Need Is Love' which includes a cover of The Beetles 'All You Need Is Love', and his song Nowhere Else (Formerly known as Fake Love). On April 24th, 2019, 909 was released alongside an official video, with more to come in 2019. Along with writing and producing his own music, Ng produces for the rapper ATO. In 2014, they initially released an EP together titled 'Intro'. The first single officially released together, titled 'Notion', was re-released along with a music video on 20 June 2016. Since then, they have released several songs together - Notion, Darkroom, Heaven, and Heights.
